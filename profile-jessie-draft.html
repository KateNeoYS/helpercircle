Notes from her employer

Drawn from Annie's observations across two years working with Jessie in their home.

Jessie Ann is Filipino, 49, and Christian. She has been working in Singapore for sixteen years across four families, and has been with Annie's family for the past two. Annie is releasing her now because the children are older (10 and 13) and Annie wants them to be more hands-on with their own routines — the family is shifting to a part-time cleaner rather than a full-time helper. Jessie is looking for her next Singapore family, and is hoping the role can focus primarily on housekeeping rather than childcare.

She speaks English and Filipino. In her time across four Singapore families, she has handled housekeeping, cooking, laundry, ironing, grocery shopping, gardening, and childcare. She cooks Asian, Chinese, Filipino, and French cuisines.

Across two years with Annie's family, three things stand out about how Jessie works.

The first is her complete ownership of the children's logistics. In Annie's words: "After two years, Jessie knows the weekly schedule better than we do; she tracks piano lessons, BJJ sessions, and basic English homework without needing reminders. On more than one occasion when I couldn't respond to messages, she didn't wait to be told: she made the call, got the kids where they needed to be, and let me know afterward. This is rare and genuinely invaluable."

The second is how she handled being sick. Annie shares: "She fell ill once during her time with us and still came in to cover the essential duties. We had not asked her to; she made that judgment herself because she didn't want to leave the family stranded. This is really appreciated."

The third is something smaller but, in Annie's words, "equally telling: Jessie does the dishes whenever she sees them, including on her days off. Nobody asked. It's simply not in her nature to walk past something that needs doing."

Jessie has cared for Annie's two children (currently 10 and 13) across the past two years — experienced across primary-school routines and the pre-teen / early-teen years. She also has experience with younger children from previous families. A small detail Annie wanted to share: Jessie always brings a gift on the children's birthdays, which the kids genuinely appreciate.

Best fit. Annie has been honest about this: "Jessie suits an organised household with one or two children where there is a reasonable level of trust and communication. She takes direction well and is very open to guidance. She naturally notices what needs doing and acts on it, so she's at her best in a family that appreciates that quality rather than one that is very prescriptive about every small task. A warm, communicative household where she is treated as a trusted individual is where she will give her best."

In her own words on what she's hoping for next: "I hope to work with a kind and respectful family who values honesty, trust, and good communication. I am committed to doing my job well and would like to build a positive and long-term relationship with my employer."

Annie's family has cats. Jessie is not comfortable with dogs.
