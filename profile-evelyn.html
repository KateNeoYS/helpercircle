Notes from her employer

Drawn from Marianne's observations across a year working with Evelyn in their home.

Evelyn is Filipino, 50, and Catholic. She has been working in Singapore since 2009, and has been with Marianne's family for the past year. Marianne's family is now leaving Singapore, so Evelyn is looking for her next family. Marianne describes her as "reliable, honest, independent, energetic, and always in a good mood."

She speaks English and Tagalog. Across her years in Singapore she has worked with a series of international families — American, German, Indian, Chinese, Italian, Korean, Pakistani, and Dutch households among them — caring for children aged five and above, through to the teenage years. In Marianne's words: "She loves children, and handles grocery shopping, housekeeping, laundry, and cooking very efficiently."

One detail Marianne shared captures how naturally Evelyn fits into their home: each morning the family gathers to plan the day's meals together, a small ritual they put down to how much they enjoy Evelyn's cooking.

Evelyn is a single mother of two grown sons, 21 and 23, both at university in the Philippines.

On cooking she's flexible and easy-going — comfortable with whatever the household needs, and happy to follow the recipes and preferences each family shares with her. She's comfortable in homes with cats or dogs.

Best fit. Marianne suggests Evelyn would best suit "a family with teenagers, or a single mother with one or two kids starting around 8 to 12 years old." Her broader experience extends across the school-age and older children she's looking to care for.

What she's hoping for. Evelyn would like to join an expat family with school-age or older children, and to keep Sundays and public holidays as her rest days.
