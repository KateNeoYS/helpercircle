Notes from her employer

Drawn from a written reference letter Anne has provided, with additional detail from earlier conversations with her.

Amie has been with Anne's family in Singapore for the past eight years, and has 17 years of experience living and working in Singapore in total. Before joining Anne's family, she was with her previous employer for five years. From Amie's own account, her first family in Singapore was Chinese, and the family directly before Anne's was Israeli. Her English — both written and spoken — is excellent, and she speaks Tagalog. She doesn't speak Mandarin.

Anne describes her as "exceptionally honest, trustworthy, and reliable." The family has entrusted her with the care of their children for many years, including periods of sole charge during school holidays while both parents were working. Amie began caring for their youngest when he was three years old and has supported the family through many stages of the children's development.

On the household side, Amie is resourceful with Singapore's day-to-day systems — familiar with local transport, shopping, and how things work. Anne writes that when their family first moved to Singapore, Amie's local knowledge was invaluable in helping them settle in and navigate everyday tasks. She plans meals, does the grocery shopping, takes the children to and from activities, and manages her own time well.

For cooking, Amie cooks Western food well and is attentive to the individual preferences of children and family members. Anne notes she has a good understanding of likes and dislikes, follows new recipes comfortably, and adapts meals as the household's needs change.

One honest note Anne wanted to share, in her own words: "While not naturally demonstrative, Amie is a genuinely caring and dependable person who shows her concern for others through practical actions. She is attentive to the needs of family members, reliable during difficult periods, and consistently willing to adapt and help wherever needed."

On work style, Anne writes that Amie "is dependable, works well independently, and responds well to clear routines and expectations." This dovetails with what Anne has shared in earlier conversations — Amie is confident managing established responsibilities and appreciates checking in with employers when priorities change or clarification is needed.

The family has cats, and Amie is comfortable with them. She has shared that she's not comfortable with dogs.

In Amie's own words on what she's hoping for next, she's looking for a small Western family in a condo, with children aged three and above.

One thing Anne wanted to flag honestly: Amie values her personal time on rest days, and any family considering her should be comfortable with the same arrangement Anne has had — that her time off is her own, including occasional weekend sleepovers with friends, as long as she's ready for Monday. She always communicates her plans in advance.
