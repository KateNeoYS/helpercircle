Refer a Helper
If your helper is finishing up

Help her find a good next family.

For Singapore families saying goodbye to a helper they trust — and hoping she lands somewhere good.

What this is

Helper Circle is a small platform for Singapore families to pass on helpers they've worked with directly to the next family — instead of leaving her to start over with an agency.

If you trust her, and she's transferring, we'll help her find a family who'll value her the way you did. Most referrals take 5–10 minutes of your time, and a short call.

What you'd be giving her

  1. 1
    Your honest words about her A real reference, in your voice — not a recruiter's biodata sheet. Families see exactly what made her good in your home.
  2. 2
    A direct path to her next family No agency cycle, no months of biodata circulation. She moves from your home to another known one, with continuity.
  3. 3
    No loans, no salary cuts She doesn't pay placement fees, doesn't lose months of salary to commission. The next family pays one transparent admin fee — never her.

What's expected of you

  1. 1
    A short referral form About 5–10 minutes. Her background, what she's good at, and a few honest words about your experience with her.
  2. 2
    A quick verification call 15–20 minutes, by phone or WhatsApp. We confirm a few details with you so the next family sees a "Referral verified" badge on her profile.
  3. 3
    Optionally: a short chat with the next family Only if you opt in. Some next families appreciate a brief conversation with the previous employer. You can always say no — we'll vouch on your behalf.

What this isn't

  • —Not an agency. We don't source helpers, recruit from overseas, or run a placement business.
  • —No commission on her salary. Ever. The next family pays an admin fee directly to us — she keeps her full pay.
  • —No bulk biodata. Profiles are added one at a time, only when a real family refers someone they know.
  • —No pressure on you. If a match doesn't feel right, walk away. The platform exists to make a thoughtful transition easier — not to push one through.

"We didn't want to send our helper home. Helper Circle started because there should be a better way to pass a good helper to another good family — and to make that process clearer for everyone."

— The Helper Circle founding family
