About Helper Circle

Helper Circle is a guided referral platform where families can meet employer-referred helpers already in Singapore. We are not a maid agency. Instead of charging placement fees, we support direct hiring through structured introductions, verification, and administrative coordination.

No. Helper Circle is not a maid agency. We do not recruit overseas helpers or charge placement fees. Families browse profiles, request introductions, and decide directly if there is mutual interest.

Direct Hire & Transfers

A direct hire transfer happens when a helper already working in Singapore transfers directly to a new employer instead of returning home and restarting through an agency.

Yes. Transfer hiring is a common MOM-supported process provided all work permit requirements are properly completed.

The most common reasons: the previous family is relocating overseas, their children have grown up and no longer need a helper, or an elderly family member they cared for has passed away or moved into a care facility.

Each profile on Helper Circle includes the reason for transfer, reviewed with the referring employer. We do not list helpers who left a previous role under dispute.

Pricing & Fees

Yes. Browsing profiles and requesting introductions are free. You only pay if both parties proceed with administrative support.

The admin fee covers transfer guidance and documentation support such as paperwork coordination, guidance on required documents, and administrative support throughout the transfer process.

The Helper Circle admin support fee covers our guidance and documentation support only. A few costs are paid directly to third parties and are not part of our fee:

  • MOM Work Permit application fee
  • MOM Work Permit issuance fee
  • FDW insurance
  • Security bond (if applicable)
  • Helper’s salary and monthly levy

These are billed at cost — we never mark them up.

These costs vary depending on permit requirements and insurance providers. Separating them keeps pricing transparent and avoids hidden markups.

Before a Work Permit can be issued, employers are required to put three things in place:

  • Medical insurance
  • Personal accident insurance
  • A security bond (for non-Malaysian helpers)

In practice you don’t buy these one by one. Most insurers offer a single foreign domestic worker (FDW) insurance package that already includes the security bond — so it’s one purchase, not three.

You’re free to use any MOM-compliant insurer. Commonly used providers in Singapore include Income, AIA, Great Eastern, HL Assurance and MSIG. Helper Circle doesn’t receive commissions from any insurer, so the choice is entirely yours.

Once your insurance is in place, we can guide you through the MOM transfer application and Work Permit issuance, step by step.

Good to know: Malaysian helpers don’t require a security bond. For helpers of other nationalities, the bond is usually arranged by the insurer as part of the FDW insurance package — not something you arrange on your own.

Here’s the full picture, so there are no surprises later. Only the first line is paid to Helper Circle — everything else goes directly to MOM, your insurer, or your helper.
