Notes from her employer

Drawn from Marianne's observations across one year working with Evelyn in their home.

Evelyn has been with Marianne's family in Singapore for the past year. The family is now leaving Singapore, and Evelyn is looking for her next family. Marianne describes her as "reliable, honest, independent, energetic, and always in a good mood."

Evelyn has grown-up children of her own in the Philippines. Across her time in Singapore she has mainly worked for international families and has cared for both teenagers and younger children. In Marianne's words: "She loves children and handles grocery shopping, housekeeping, laundry, and cooking very efficiently."

One thing Marianne shared about how Evelyn fits into their household captures her well. In Marianne's words: "Every morning, we check the fridge and sit together to choose the recipes for the dinner, snack, and tomorrow's lunch box for the kids. Kids and husband join the discussion from time to time and we come out altogether with a plan. Great family moment as we all like eating and love Evelyn's cooking."

Best fit. Marianne suggests Evelyn would best suit "a family with teenagers, or a single mother with one or two kids starting around 8 to 12 years old." Her broader experience does extend to younger children too, but the calibration above reflects where Marianne thinks Evelyn is at her best.

In Evelyn's own account. She has worked in Singapore since 2009, with a series of international families — American, German, Indian, Chinese, Italian, Korean, Pakistani and Dutch households among them. Across those years she has cared for children of nearly every age, from newborns and toddlers to teenagers. She is a single mother of two grown sons, 21 and 23, both at university in the Philippines. (She is 50.)

Evelyn is Catholic and speaks English and Tagalog. She's comfortable in homes with cats or dogs. On cooking she's flexible and easy-going — comfortable with whatever the household needs, and happy to follow the recipes and preferences each family shares with her.

What she's hoping for. Evelyn would like to join an expat family with older children — from around ten and up — and to keep Sundays and public holidays as her rest days.

Full employment history available to interested families, with Evelyn's consent.
