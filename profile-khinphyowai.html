Elderly care

Her first household was the family’s grandfather. She gave him his medicines, cooked his dinner, and went with him wherever he went. He managed daily life independently, so her role was to be alongside him, keep the household running, and be there if he felt faint. She also cared for the family’s Shiba Inu.

Medication reminders Cooking for one Accompanying outings Companionship

Childcare

In her current household there are four children. A girl of ten, a girl of six, a boy of five, and the youngest, who is now one year and three months old. She has had the youngest since he was a baby. She does the school runs for the three older children, takes the younger two to taekwondo, badminton and swimming, and cares for the little one at home.

Baby & toddler care School drop-off & pick-up Activities & classes Cooking for children Ages 0 to 10

Why she is moving on

There are two helpers in the household at the moment. Khin’s own role is the youngest child, whom she took at one week old and slept beside every night, while she and the other helper share the cooking, housekeeping and school routines for the three older children.

The other helper leaves in February, and the family are not replacing her. From then, all of it would be Khin’s alone: the youngest, the three older children, the cooking, cleaning, laundry and the whole household. She would rather do fewer things properly than everything at once.

Her employer has been open with her about the change and is in no hurry for her to go. Khin is welcome to stay until she finds a family that suits her well.

Family she may suit

Khin Phyo Wai has done two quite different jobs well inside the same family. What she is asking for now is a role she can do thoroughly.

  • A household with an elderly member who would value daily company, medication and cooking. This is the work she did in her first household.
  • A family with older or school-age children, including the daily run to school and activities.
  • Households with a dog.
  • Her English is limited, so a household where that is workable, or where some Burmese is spoken.

She has real experience with babies, including one she cared for from a week old. What she is not looking for is sole charge of an infant alongside a full household, which is the situation she is leaving now. Her strengths are care, warmth and reliability rather than exacting housekeeping, and a household that wants an immaculate home above all else would not be the right fit.

Her employer’s recommendation

Pearlyn has employed Khin since 2023, first in her father-in-law’s household and then in her own. She referred Khin to us herself, and is happy to speak with families considering her.

  • “Very kind hearted.” However naughty the children are, Pearlyn says she forgives and forgets, and is never harsh with them.
  • “Very street smart.” Give her an address and a task, says Pearlyn, and she will get there and get it done, even if she loses her way on the route.
  • Optimistic and easy to work with. She does not argue, takes feedback well, and stays positive.
  • Proactive with the baby. When the youngest was waking every three to four hours, Khin offered to keep him with her at night so the family could rest.
  • Trusted with the whole household. Pearlyn says Khin can manage all four children on her own, and the family take her with them on holidays.

Pearlyn wants Khin to find the right family rather than the quickest one, and has said there is no rush at her end.
