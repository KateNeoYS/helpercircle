Notes from her employer

Written from what Anne shared about working with Amie since 2018.

Amie has been with Anne's family in Singapore since 2018, around seven years. Her English — both written and spoken — is excellent, and she speaks Tagalog. Her first employer in Singapore was Chinese, though she doesn't speak Mandarin herself.

In the day-to-day, Amie plans meals, does the grocery shopping, takes the children to and from activities, and manages her own time well. She has good Western cooking and is comfortable following recipes.

Anne is honest about how Amie works best: she's a helper rather than a home manager. She thrives in households where the family is around to give guidance and answer questions — a working couple where someone is reachable, for instance. On cleaning specifically, Anne notes that Amie does well with a clear list rather than being expected to anticipate what needs doing. Anne's straightforward about it: "she's not a mind-reader."

For childcare, Amie has cared for Anne's children since they were young through to their current ages, and Anne has felt comfortable leaving the children with her for up to two days and one night. Households where parents travel for work for many days at a stretch, leaving Amie in sole charge, would likely be a stretch.

She has been flexible when Anne's family has asked her to work for birthday parties or when they've had guests. The family has cats, and Amie is comfortable with them. Dogs are something for a next family to check.

One thing Anne wanted to flag honestly: Amie values her personal time on rest days, and any family considering her should be comfortable with the same arrangement Anne has had — that her time off is her own, including occasional weekend sleepovers with friends, as long as she's ready for Monday. She always communicates her plans in advance.
