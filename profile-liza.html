Notes from her employer

Drawn from her current employer's referral. Length of time together still to confirm.

Liza's current employer has shared a warm referral. They describe her as "a genuinely kind, warm and responsible person who is great especially with younger children."

What stands out most is how she keeps young children engaged. In their words: "She's incredibly creative when it comes to crafts, games and finding ways to keep younger kids engaged and entertained without simply relying on screens." She is also independent, health-conscious and active, often walking when she can, and takes pride in looking after both herself and the children in her care.

At this family, Liza has handled cleaning, laundry, and the daily grocery shopping (with weekly lists prepared for their Redmart orders), cooking for special dietary needs including a gluten allergy and dairy intolerance, school pickup, helping care for their small dog, and the practical running of the home.

Liza's experience runs deep. She has worked as a helper since 1996, nearly thirty years, first in Singapore and then for eighteen years in Hong Kong with Chinese, French, British and Danish families. She moved back to Singapore in 2018 with the Danish family she had been working for, and has been here since. Across those years she has cared for children of every age, from newborns and toddlers up to teenagers around sixteen.

Her employer sums it up simply: "We have a lot of appreciation for who she is as a person, and we would be very happy to see her join a kind family where her strengths are a great match."

What she's hoping for. In her own words, Liza is looking for "someone who matches my strength and treats me as a human being," in a home where she can look after the children, the house, and the family's day-to-day needs.

Liza is 54 and Catholic, and speaks English and Tagalog.
