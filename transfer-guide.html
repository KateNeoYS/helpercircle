After you both decide to go ahead

The transfer, step by step.

Once two families agree to proceed, the helper moves across through MOM's own process. Here is what each side does — in plain language.

A transfer in Singapore is handled directly between the two families through MOM's FDW eService, with the helper signing alongside the new family. There is no overseas sourcing and no agency in the middle.

Helper Circle's part is to walk alongside you both — confirming everyone genuinely agrees, making sure her medical is current, pointing you to the right MOM steps in the right order, and helping the two families settle on a handover date. Each family logs in under their own SingPass and submits their own steps. We guide; we don't transact on anyone's behalf.

The family saying goodbye

If you're the current employer.

You stay responsible for her upkeep right up until the new Work Permit is issued — so these steps keep things clean on your side.

1

Make sure her 6-monthly medical is current.

If she's due, send her for her six-monthly medical examination before the transfer goes through. The new family will need it confirmed before they can finish their steps.

We'll remind you when this is due and help you find a clinic.
2

Approve the transfer request in FDW eService.

Once the new family applies, MOM emails you a request to approve. Wait for that email, then log in to FDW eService under your own SingPass to approve it.

3

Keep paying the levy until the new permit issues.

Your levy billing continues up to the day the new family issues the new Work Permit. The moment that happens, the old permit is automatically cancelled and your levy billing stops.

4

Cut up the old Work Permit card.

After the new permit is issued, cut the cancelled card in half and discard it so it can't be misused. MOM discharges your security bond about a week after she starts with the new family, and will let you know by post.

Good news for you: because she's transferring rather than going home, there's no air ticket to buy.

The family welcoming her in

If you're the new employer.

These steps run in order. The first two need to happen at least 30 days before her current Work Permit expires — so it's worth starting early.

1

Apply for the new Work Permit.

Apply through MOM's FDW eService under your own SingPass. If approved, MOM emails you the In-Principle Approval (IPA) — you can also download the IPA and the Declaration form from the eService.

First time doing this? We'll sit with you and walk through the screens.
2

Buy the security bond and insurance.

Follow the actions listed on your IPA: purchase the security bond (required for non-Malaysian helpers) and buy medical and personal accident insurance for her.

We'll help you compare insurance options so you're not guessing.
3

Sign the Declaration form — both of you.

Print the Declaration form from FDW eService. It needs to be signed by you and by the helper herself. Her signature here is part of how the process confirms she agrees to the move.

4

Agree a handover date with the current family.

Talk to the current employer and settle on a transfer date that works for everyone — most of all for her.

We're happy to help coordinate this between the two families.
5

On the day: issue the permit, hand her the Temporary Permit.

On the agreed date, use FDW eService to issue the new Work Permit, upload the signed Declaration form, and print the Temporary Work Permit. Give that to her so she can start with you — her old permit cancels automatically.

Useful to have on hand

The numbers families ask about.

Security bond

S$5,000

Required for each non-Malaysian helper. Held by MOM, discharged after she starts.

Monthly levy

S$300

Or S$60 at the concessionary rate for households with a young child, an elderly person, or a person with disabilities.

Key deadline

30 days

The application and IPA should be done at least 30 days before her current permit expires.

Air ticket on transfer

None

Because she's transferring rather than returning home, the goodbye family doesn't buy a ticket.

Figures reflect MOM's published rates and may change — we'll always point you to the current MOM guidance rather than relying on a snapshot.

Where we sit

What coordinating does — and doesn't mean.

  • We don't log in to MOM for you — every step is submitted by the family it belongs to, under their own SingPass.
  • We don't hold the helper, take a cut of her salary, or charge her anything.
  • We don't source helpers from overseas or keep a roster waiting to be placed.
  • We do stay reachable throughout, so neither family is reading MOM pages alone at 11pm.
