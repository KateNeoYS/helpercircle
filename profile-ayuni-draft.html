Notes from her employer

Drawn from Rougeron’s recommendation. We’ll round this out with her words, a few details to confirm, and Ayuni’s own.

Rougeron has given a warm, detailed recommendation for Ayuni, an Indonesian helper with more than 18 years of experience in Singapore, who worked for her expat family here.

Her reference is glowing. She describes a strong work ethic and someone who managed their large home, nine bedrooms over four storeys, along with the garden and cars, all with real attention to detail and to a high standard. In the kitchen she is proficient and creative across Chinese, some Thai, Vietnamese, French and Western cooking, with a good hand for meal planning.

She is also experienced with small children and a small dog, and is comfortable with the marketing and wet-market. She is a meticulous ironer too: with Rougeron’s husband travelling often for business, she looked after his shirts and packed his luggage for his trips. She describes her as a pleasure to work with: positive, professional and a good communicator, and extremely honest, with no loan.

What stands out most is her character. Ayuni stayed and supported the family through their hardest time in Singapore, when Rougeron’s husband was unwell in hospital, remaining with them until they moved back to their home country. Throughout, she never once asked for more pay or anything in return.

Ayuni is moving on because the family she is with now is not the right fit. Her previous employer, Rougeron, who valued her deeply, is the one helping her find a better long-term home.

What Ayuni is hoping for is straightforward: a household where she can look after children, or care for a dog, and settle for the long term.

Rougeron’s hope is simply to see Ayuni settle with a family who will value her as hers did.
